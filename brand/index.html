Fonts

Two faces. Both free.

Portfolio uses two typefaces. Both are on Google Fonts under the SIL Open Font License. Install in your design tool, link from your web project, or pull via Fontsource on a CDN.

Aa
Momo Trust Display
Display + wordmark. Rounded geometric sans-serif by TYPE Associates (2024). Regular weight only on Google Fonts—Medium weight used for the wordmark is browser-synthesized.
Open on Google Fonts ↗
Aa
Inter
Body + UI. Variable font by Rasmus Andersson. Use Regular (400) for body and Medium (500) for subheadings, buttons, and uppercase eyebrow labels.
Open on Google Fonts ↗

Typography

Two fonts. Tight tracking.

Momo Trust Display for display and wordmark. Inter for body and UI. Negative tracking across all running sizes—about −5% of font size for display, −2.5% for body. Uppercase eyebrows use positive 0.18em.

Wordmark

The name is the mark.

Momo Trust Display Medium. Capital P, lowercase rest. Tracking tighter than the hero (~−6% of font size). No icon, no lockup, no decoration. Light on dark, dark on light. Two versions only.

Buttons

Two styles only.

Primary (filled) and Ghost (outline). 12px radius. Inter Medium 15px. The filled side stays constant across modes—wordmark color on ground color, swapped.

Surface system

Three layers, no shadows.

Ground, section, hairline. Tonal architecture only—no drop shadows, no inset shadows, no card outlines. The combination of tonal step plus 0.5px hairline creates the section seam.

Voice & tone

How Portfolio writes.

Stripped down, direct, and serious without being stiff. The voice reads like a pitch from someone who respects your time and trusts your intelligence.

Direct
Short sentences. Active voice. No hedging or corporate filler. "Now's the time"—not "we believe the current moment presents an opportunity."
Serious
The stakes are real and the tone reflects it. No exclamation points, no emojis, no forced optimism. Urgency comes from substance, not punctuation.
Invitational
Despite the gravity, the voice is warm and welcoming. "Get in touch"—not "Apply now." The CTA is a handshake, not a form submission.
Professional
Written for peers, not donors or volunteers. Assumes intelligence and agency. "Professionals from across disciplines"—people with real careers and real expertise.
A special projects studio for democracy

The single canonical tagline. Use as written. Do not pluralize, abbreviate, or substitute.

Punctuation

Em-dashes set tight
No spaces around the em-dash, ever. Write "like this—not like this." The MIT/Chicago convention, not AP. Use em-dashes for clause-level pivots and parenthetical breaks; apply the same rule in titles, body, UI copy, and email.
Oxford commas
Always.
Curly quotes
Not straight. Single quotes for contractions ('it's), double quotes for actual quotation ("like this").
No exclamation points
Outside dialogue. The voice creates urgency through substance, not punctuation.
Do
"We take high-variance ideas and move them from concept to operating entity in weeks."
"The times are weird. What is missing is not more organizations doing conventional work."
"Now's the time."
Don't
"Join our amazing community of changemakers!"
"We're excited to announce our groundbreaking new initiative!"
"Together, we can make a difference!!!"

On AI

Portfolio is an AI-forward studio, but the brand does not advertise this. The orientation lives in what the studio does, not how it dresses. Avoid copy that names AI as a feature or differentiator.

On the broader portfolio

Each project carries its own brand identity, team, and operating tempo. Portfolio's outward voice does not list its projects, name them publicly, or describe them in detail on portfolio.sc. Project names appear only on their own surfaces.
